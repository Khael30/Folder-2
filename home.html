What is Chiropractic?

“Chiropractic” is a healthcare discipline that focuses on diagnosing, treating, and preventing
mechanical disorders of the musculoskeletal system, particularly the spine. The primary goal
of chiropractic care is to enhance the functionality of the nervous system and improve overall
health without the use of drugs or surgery


Key Principles of Chiropractic Care:

1."Spinal Alignment and Health" - Chiropractors use manual adjustments to correct spinal
      misalignments (subluxations). Proper alignment of the spine is believed to improve the
      body's physical function and overall health.

2.“ Holistic Approach” - Chiropractic care emphasizes a holistic approach to health
      considering the patients overall well-being. This includes lifestyle advice, nutrition, and
      exercise recommendations alongside spinal adjustments.

3.“ Natural Healing” - By ensuring that the spine and nervous system are functioning
      optimally, chiropractic care aims to support the body natural healing processes.

Benefits of Chiropractic Care:

  • “Pain Relief”: Effective for treating acute and chronic back pain, neck pain, and
           headaches.
  • “Improved Mobility”: Helps restore normal motion and function to joints.
  • “Enhanced Nervous System Function”: By removing interference from the nervous
           system, it supports better communication between the brain and the body.
  • “Non-Invasive Treatment”: Offers a non-surgical and drug-free approach to health care.

    • Common Conditions Treated by Chiropractors:

  • Back Pain
  • Neck Pain
  • Headaches and Migraines
  • Sciatica - Scoliosis
  • Whiplash and Auto Injuries
  • Sports Injuries

    • What to Expect During a Chiropractic Visit:

    1. “Initial Consultation and Examination”- The chiropractor will take a detailed medical
          history and perform a physical examination, which may include diagnostic tests like X-rays.

    2. “Treatment Plan”- Based on the findings, the chiropractor will develop a personalized
          treatment plan. This may include spinal adjustments, physical therapy, lifestyle advice, and
          other modalities.

    3. “Spinal Adjustments”- The core treatment involves manual adjustments to the spine to
          correct misalignments and improve function.

    4. “Ongoing Care”- Follow-up visits and continuous care are often recommended to maintain
          spinal health and prevent recurrence of symptoms
