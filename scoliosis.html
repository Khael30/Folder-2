SCOLIOSIS TREATMENT

In our clinic, we understand that Scoliosis, a disease that makes the spine curve sideways by more than 10° , can happen at any age, mostly at the teenage years just before puberty when growth is most rapid. Scoliosis is the spine rotation which results in the tilting of the rib cage or back. While fusion is the most extreme case, most of them don't need it, especially in the early stages.

The scoliosis in adolescents should be monitored constantly because the curves can grow more than expected, and the old idea that the curves improve with age has been proven wrong, the adults can also have their curves worsen over time. The adults with scoliosis are observed to have their curves to become deeper at the average rate of 1. 47° per year. The first and the most real assessment is the basis for the right treatment that is specific to scoliosis, traction therapies, and bracing. The scoliosis can be treated effectively if it is taken care of properly so many cases of scoliosis can be treated without surgery. If you or a friend of yours has scoliosis, we are here to assist you with the full examination and a lot of treatment options.

Adolescent idiopathic scoliosis, the most prevalent type, has no known reason and usually occurs during the rapid growth phases of kids, mainly from the ages of 10 to 13. The cause of this phenomenon remains a mystery, however, the factors such as age, curve size, and skeletal maturity can be the predictors of the occurrence of the increase in the curve. The evaluation and monitoring of small curves are even more important than the usual ones to reduce the risks. Adult-onset scoliosis, which is a common problem, usually occurs in people whose spine has degenerated over time. The force of gravity and time show to be the cause of the degenerative changes, which then are complemented by each other to become the reason of the aggravation of the curvature over time.

Poor posture or even the traumatic incidents can worsen scoliosis and other misalignments, which proves that the regular chiropractic care is very essential. Some of the therapy techniques specifically designed for scoliosis can help a lot in the maintenance and management of the condition.

Treatment Options:
  • Chiropractic adjustments
  • Corrective exercises
  • Spinal bracing (if necessary)
  • Postural education and awareness
